Brazilian Lemonade

Kill The Heat with this cool beverage

Unlike traditional lemonade made with lemon juice, Brazilian Lemonade uses fresh limes. It has a unique and refreshing taste from combining tangy limes with sweetened condensed milk, giving it a smooth, creamy texture and rich luscious flavor.

Traditional Brazilian Lemonade is made by blending the entire lime, skin and all, then strained through a sieve. While all that lime zest does add a lot of flavor, I still find it to be bitter because of the pith..

Ingredients

  • Cold water
  • Granulated sugar ‐ The sugar helps to balance out the tanginess of the limes.
  • Fresh lime juice ‐ You'll need about 5 or 6 limes, depending on the size. Choose limes with smooth, thin skin ‐ they tend to be the juiciest and have less chance of making the drink bitter.
  • Lime zest ‐ Adds more lime flavor to the lemonade.
  • Sweetened condensed milk ‐ Adds the rich, creamy texture and flavor.
  • Ice

Steps

  1. Scrub and wash the limes really well, then dry them. Zest one lime, then juice all of them. (Itâ€™s much easier to zest a lime before juicing it.)
  2. Stir together the cold water and sugar. Add the lime juice and zest, sugar-water, and sweetened condensed milk to a blender. Blend until smooth. Taste test and add more sugar if necessary.
  3. Pour in glasses over ice and garnish with a lime slice. Enjoy immediately.
